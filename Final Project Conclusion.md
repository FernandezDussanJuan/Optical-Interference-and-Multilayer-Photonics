# Final Project Conclusion

This project developed a coherent and progressively structured computational study of optical interference phenomena, bridging analytical wave optics and numerical simulation.

Starting from simple two-wave interference, the study advanced toward increasingly complex systems including:
* **Thin films**
* **Fabry-Pérot resonators**
* **Arbitrary multilayer stacks** (modeled through the Transfer Matrix Method).

---

## 🛡️ Numerical Validation
The numerical implementation was rigorously validated by:
* **Explicit verification of energy conservation:** Ensuring $R + T = 1$.
* **Analytical comparison:** Verification of Bragg mirror stop-band width.
* **Physical consistency checks:** Performed at each stage of the development.

---

## 🎯 Key Results
The final model successfully reproduces:
* **Resonant cavity behavior**
* **Constructive and destructive interference patterns**
* **Photonic band gap formation** in periodic structures.

---

## 🚀 Future Work
This work establishes a solid foundation for future exploration of:
* Photonic crystals
* Optical coatings
* Nanophotonics
* Integrated photonic devices

The project demonstrates the power of combining **theoretical electromagnetism** with **computational physics** to analyze and design complex optical systems.
